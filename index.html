Ioana Bica

University of Oxford
The Alan Turing Institute
I am a second-year PhD student at the University of Oxford and at the Alan Turing Institute. My PhD research focuses on building machine learning methods for causal inference and individualised treatment effect estimation from observational data. In particular, I have worked on developping methods capable of estimating the heterogeneous effects of time-dependent treatments, thus enabling us to determine when to give treatments to patients and how to select among multiple treatments over time. Recently, I have started working on methods for understanding and modelling clinical decision making through causality, inverse reinforcement learning and imitation learning. Prior to my PhD, I have completed a Bachelor’s degree and a Master’s degree in Computer Science at the University of Cambridge where she has worked on multi-modal data integration and unsupervised learning for genomics data.
